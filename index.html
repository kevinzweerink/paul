Paul Jaskunas is a professor at the Maryland Institute College of Art and the author of the novel Hidden. Selected by Amazon as one of the best 50 books of the year, Hidden received the Friends of American Writers Award for Fiction and has been published widely in Europe. His story “The Market for Virgins” was published as an e-book by Amazon’s StoryFront, a new imprint for short fiction. At MICA he has taught courses on nineteenth-century American literature, the postwar American novel, contemporary fiction, and literary naturalism. He also leads writing workshops on short fiction, creative nonfiction, and graphic literature. In the past, Paul has worked as a journalist and a publicist. He lives in Maryland and can be reached at pjaskunas@mica.edu.

Publications

Hidden

Six years after her attack, Maggie Wilson receives a call from the prosecutor who put her husband in jail after Maggie identified him as the man who nearly killed her. Told that another inmate has confessed to the crime, Maggie plunges into memories of her stormy marriage to her college sweetheart, Nate Duke. Secluded in the old farmhouse that was her marital home, Maggie pores through trial transcripts, old journals, and photo albums, recreating the history of their courtship, trying fruitlessly to remember the events of that fateful night.
